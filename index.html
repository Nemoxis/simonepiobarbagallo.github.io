About

Research Profile

I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science (40th cycle) at the University of Catania, conducting research within the Archeomatica Lab under Prof. Filippo Stanco. My work sits at the convergence of Artificial Intelligence, Digital Humanities, and Cultural Heritage, developing intelligent systems that combine Large Language Models, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), Computer Vision, and 3D technologies to create immersive and accessible cultural heritage experiences.

Alongside AI-driven cultural heritage systems, I investigate Game Studies and Serious Games as vehicles for cultural knowledge transmission. My research explores how game design principles — narrative agency, immersive mechanics, and interactive storytelling — can serve as powerful frameworks for engaging audiences with historical and archaeological content, contributing to the fields of game-based learning, game literacy, and transmedia education within the Digital Humanities.
